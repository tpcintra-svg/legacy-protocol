The Problem

Digital legacies have no trustless path to the right hands.

Whistleblowers, heirs, and key holders all face the same structural gap: there's no way to guarantee sensitive material reaches someone else, only if something happens to you — without trusting a person, a company, or a single point of failure.

01

The Whistleblower Vulnerability

Informants and journalists have no trustless way to secure an "insurance vault." If they're compromised, the truth they were holding disappears with them.

02

The Single-Beneficiary Bottleneck

Most succession tools support one static recipient. There's no way to release the same material to several people — family, lawyers, press — at once.

03

Metadata Harvesting

Centralized escrow leaks who's connected to what. An attacker watching the chain shouldn't be able to tell that you have anything locked away at all.

How It Works

One non-custodial vault. Multiple recipients. Your terms.

A creator locks encrypted material in a smart contract, names who can claim it, and sets the window for disputing a false claim. Nothing here requires us, or anyone, to hold a key on your behalf.

Multi-recipient vaults
Name up to 50 recipients per vault, each able to claim independently once the vault unlocks — no single point of failure on the receiving end.
Live
Signature-verified claims
Claims are authorized by a cryptographic signature bound to the claimant's address, the vault, and a one-time nonce — never a value exposed on-chain that anyone could copy and reuse.
Live
Creator veto window
A challenge window from 72 hours to 30 days, chosen by the creator. A single signature from the creator cancels a false claim outright.
Live
365-day recovery window
Once claimed, a recipient gets a full year of free re-access — protection against a corrupted download or a lost device, at no extra cost.
Live
On-chain governance
Protocol-level changes require 3-of-6 independent approvals and a mandatory 14-day public delay before they take effect. No single key, including ours, can act alone.
Live
Private discovery
The public app exposes no list of active vaults or recipients. True zero-knowledge proof of recipiency — so even direct chain inspection reveals nothing — is a research-stage goal, not yet implemented.
Partial
Decentralized storage network
Sharding the decryption key across independent, blind-routing nodes via Shamir's Secret Sharing. Today, vaults can reference any storage a creator chooses, including existing decentralized options.
Planned
Multi-stage claims adjudication
Automated document review, a human review tier, and a decentralized jury for contested claims at scale. The chain already supports the final signed-claim step; the review stages do not yet exist.

Invariant Honey Pot Planned

1M
LGY locked forever
Day 1
Removed from circulation
1,000,000 LGY tokens are locked inside the contract on launch day and permanently removed from circulating supply. They serve as a live open bounty: anyone who finds a way to extract them has proven a critical vulnerability on-chain — no application, no committee, no CVE process. The math decides.

If someone does extract them, they earned it. It means they found something we missed — and showed us on-chain, in the most honest way possible. We consider that a fair trade.

Because these tokens can never be sold or moved under normal conditions, their dollar value grows in step with LGY. As the network expands, the bounty becomes more valuable — automatically raising the economic incentive to find flaws, without any action from the team.
